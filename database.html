Marijuana

About
Marijuana is the name of the dried leaves, flowers, stems, and seeds from the Cannabis sativa or Cannabis indica plant. The main active chemical is tetrahydrocannabinol often referred to as THC. Marijuana is the most commonly used illegal drug in the US.
Effects
Effects include altered senses, altered sense of time, changes in mood, impaired body movement, difficulty with thinking and problem-solving, impaired memory, hallucinations , breathing problems, etc.
Sources
Natinal Institute of Drug Abuse
Live Science

Cocaine

About
Cocaine is a powerfully addictive stimulant drug made from the leaves of the coca plant native to South America. This drug can be used for legitimate reasons by health care officials such as local anesthesia for some surgeries. However, recreational cocaine is illegal.
Effects
Effects include paranoia, emotional swings, insomnia followed by hypersomnia, anxiety, short attention span, hyperstimulation and energy levels, bursts of elevated mood and euphoria, lethargy and introversion, Irritability, hallucinations, loss of appetite, etc.
Sources
Natinal Institute of Drug Abuse
The Recovery Village

Xanax

About
Xanax is a Central Nervous System (CNS) depressant. These drugs are useful in treating proper health problems but throughout the years people have started abusing the drug, or attaining it for recreational use without a prescription.
Effects
Effects include seizures, shakiness, anxiety, agitation, insomnia, overactive reflexes, increased heart rate, hallucinations, severe cravings, etc.
Sources
Natinal Institute of Drug Abuse

Meth

About
Methamphetamine is a stimulant drug usually used as a white, bitter-tasting powder or a pill or as crystal methamphetamine which looks like glass fragments. The drug can be inhaled, swallowed, snorted, or injected.
Effects
Effects include increased wakefulness and physical activity, decreased appetite, faster breathing, rapid and/or irregular heartbeat, increased blood pressure and body temperature,, extreme weight loss, severe dental problems, intense itching, skin sores, anxiety, confusion, sleeping problems, violent behavior, etc.
Sources
Natinal Institute of Drug Abuse
The Recovery Village

Tobacco

About
Tobacco is a plant grown for its leaves, which are dried and fermented. Tobacco contains nicotine which is the main addictive substance. Tobacco can be smokes, chewed, or sniffed.
Effects
Effects can include irritability, problems paying attention, trouble sleeping, increased appetite, and powerful cravings for tobacco.
Sources
Natinal Institute of Drug Abuse

LSD

About
LSD is one of the most powerful mind-altering chemicals. It is a clear or white odorless material made from lysergic acid, which is found in a fungus. LSD can be swallowed as pills or liquid, or absorbed through the lining of the mouth with drug-soaked papers.
Effects
Effects include increased heart rate, nausea, intensified feelings and sensory experiences, changes in sense of time, increased blood pressure, loss of appetite, dry mouth, sleep problems, spiritual experiences, feelings of relaxation, uncoordinated movements, excessive sweating, panic, paranoia, psychosis, bizarre behaviors, etc.
Sources
Natinal Institute of Drug Abuse

Heroin

About
Heroin is an opioid drug made from morphine, a natural substance taken from the seed pod of the various opium poppy plants. People inject, sniff, snort, or smoke heroin.
Effects
Effects include dry mouth, warm flushing of the skin, heavy feeling in the arms and legs, nausea and vomiting, severe itching, clouded mental functioning, collapsed veins for people who inject the drug, damaged tissue inside the nose for people who sniff or snort it, infection of the heart lining and valves, liver and kidney disease, lung complications, mental disorders, etc.
Sources
Natinal Institute of Drug Abuse
Lakeview Behavioral Health

Alcohol

About
Alcohol is a colorless liquid. It is a widely available substance and extremely easy to get if you’re over 21. At one point in the US it was a banned substance. Most people drink it in moderation but others tend to abuse it and become addicted.
Effects
Effects include trouble concentrating, loss of coordination, loss of critical judgement, dulled perception, mood swings, reduced core body temperature, raised blood pressure, passing out, vomiting, memory loss, alcoholic hepatitis, liver fibrosis, cancer, high blood pressure, cardiomyopathy, stroke, irregular heart beat, etc.
Sources
Centers for Disease Control and Prevention
Alcohol.ORG

Steroids

About
Anabolic steroids are synthetic, or human-made, variations of the male sex hormone testosterone. Steroids are commonly illegally used as performance enhancing drugs. They can be used to build up muscle most commonly by weight lifters or bodybuilders. Steroids can be taken orally, injected into the muscles, or applied to the skin as a gel or cream.
Effects
Effects include paranoia, jealousy, extreme irritability and aggression, delusions, impaired judgment, kidney problems or failure, liver damage and tumors, enlarged heart, high blood pressure, changes in blood cholesterol, increased risk of blood clots, etc.
Sources
National Institute of Drug Abuse
